Government-wide Personnel Spending Tool

Connecting USAspending and federal employment data to visualize how personnel obligations across the federal governments budget translate to staffing across the government.

How does agency spending on personnel compare?

In the first two quarters of fiscal year 2017 (FY17), a total of $144 million dollars in personnel compensation was obligated by the largest 24 federal agencies (referred to as CFO Act Agencies*). Figure 1 shows how government-wide personnel compensation and benefits spending is distributed across these agencies, with the largest amount of personnel spending dedicated to the Department of Defense followed by the Department of Agriculture.

Figure 1: Personnel Budgets Across Government

Where are employees located?

CFO Act Agencies employ 1.99 million people across the nation, with the largest number of employees located in the District of Columbia, California, Virginia, Maryland, and Texas. Twenty three percent, or 405,228, CFO Act Agency employees are located in either the District of Columbia, Maryland, or Virginia.

How this analysis was conducted:

This analysis was conducted using federal agency obligations data reported to USAspending.gov, which is available to the public, and agency employment data made available by the Office of Personnel Management at FedScope.OPM.gov. Personnel obligations data was used to identify the cost of personnel compensation across the government and for each agency. FedScope employment data was used to verify the personnel data reported to USAspending and evaluate the distribution of employees across the country. Fedscope data was also used to determine the composition of government employees, as well as for the agency specific staff profiles.
